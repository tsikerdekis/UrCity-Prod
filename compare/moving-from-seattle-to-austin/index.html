Moving from Seattle to Austin: Complete Relocation Guide [2026]

Keep the tech salary, lose the rain and high housing costs. Austin offers 31% lower cost of living with no state income tax.

Updated June 28, 2026

Introduction

Seattle and Austin are two of America’s top tech hubs — but Austin offers dramatically lower costs while maintaining a robust tech scene. If you’re tired of Seattle’s housing prices and rainy winters, Austin might be your answer.

Culture & Lifestyle

What You’ll Miss from Seattle

  • Mountains and hiking (Rainier, Cascades)
  • Coffee culture
  • Mild summer weather
  • Progressive politics
  • Water access (Puget Sound, lakes)

What You’ll Gain in Austin

  • 228 sunny days per year
  • Live music capital of the world
  • BBQ and Tex-Mex
  • More affordable everything
  • No state income tax

Best Neighborhoods in Austin for Seattle Transplants

  1. East Austin — Trendy, artsy, most like Capitol Hill
  2. South Congress — Walkable shops and restaurants
  3. Mueller — Planned community with parks
  4. Downtown Austin — Urban, like South Lake Union

Is It Worth It?

Yes — if you prefer sun to rain.

Both cities have no state income tax and strong tech economies. Austin is significantly cheaper (31% lower cost of living) and much sunnier. The main trade-offs are summer heat and less outdoor scenery.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will my salary go down?

Possibly. Tech salaries in Austin are typically 10–20% lower than Seattle. However, the cost savings more than offset the difference.

Is Austin’s tech scene as good as Seattle’s?

Different but robust. Seattle has Microsoft and Amazon headquarters. Austin has Apple, Google, Meta, Tesla, Dell, and thousands of startups.

What’s the biggest culture shock?

The heat and the driving. Austin summers are extreme (100°F+). And unlike Seattle’s decent public transit, Austin is car-dependent.

How’s the outdoor scene?

Good but different. Austin has Lady Bird Lake, Barton Springs, and Hill Country hiking. But it’s not the Pacific Northwest.
