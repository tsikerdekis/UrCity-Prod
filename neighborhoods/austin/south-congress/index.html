South Congress (SoCo)

Guide to South Congress — Austin's trendy shopping and dining district.

South Congress (SoCo) Neighborhood Guide

South Congress Avenue is Austin’s most iconic street — a stretch of boutique shops, food trailers, and live music venues that captures the city’s “Keep Austin Weird” spirit.

Cost of Living

SoCo is one of Austin’s pricier neighborhoods, but you’re paying for character and walkability.

Median Rent (2BR)$2,800/month

Walkability

  • Walk Score: 88 (Very Walkable)
  • Transit Score: 65 (Good Transit)
  • Bike Score: 82 (Very Bikeable)

Things to Do

  • South Congress Avenue — Shopping and dining
  • Allen Boots — Iconic cowboy boot store
  • Home Slice Pizza — New York-style pizza
  • The Continental Club — Legendary music venue
  • Barton Springs Pool — Nearby spring-fed pool

Who Lives Here?

Artists, musicians, young professionals, and longtime Austinites who value character over convenience.

Pros and Cons

Pros:

  • Unique, eclectic vibe
  • Great restaurants and bars
  • Walkable
  • Strong community feel

Cons:

  • Expensive
  • Touristy on weekends
  • Traffic on Congress Avenue
  • Limited parking
